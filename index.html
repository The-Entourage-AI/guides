Step 1

Document Everything

Step 1: Write Down Everything You Do

Before you touch any AI tool, write down your processes. All of them.

Real story: A client thought they had a simple 6-step process. When we actually wrote it down, it was 47 steps. Nobody on the team could follow all of them.

How to write it: Pretend you're explaining the task to someone who has never worked at your company. Every click, every login, every decision.

📋 Template
What to document for each process:
Field What to Write
Process Name e.g., "New Client Onboarding"
Trigger What starts this process?
Steps Every single action, numbered
Tools Used Every software involved
Decision Points Where does a human need to decide?
Time to Complete How long on average?
Common Mistakes Where do people mess this up?
Bad vs Good Example
❌ Bad
Bad documentation: "Check emails and reply to customers."
✅ Good
Good documentation:
  1. Log into Gmail (operations@company.com)
  2. Filter by "Unread"
  3. Check if sender exists in CRM
  4. If yes: open their record, note account status
  5. If no: create new contact
  6. Categorise the email (support, sales, billing)
  7. Pick the right reply template
  8. Personalise and send
  9. Log the interaction in CRM
Start with your top 10 most time-consuming processes. Don't try to do all of them at once.
Key Takeaway: If you can't write it down, you can't automate it.

Step 5

Deploy Without The Mess

Step 5: Roll It Out Without Breaking Things

You've built it. Now you need to put it into your business without causing chaos.

Three things to get right:
1. Check the AI's work

Simple rule: If the output goes to a customer or outside your company, a human checks it first. For internal stuff (sorting emails, logging data), let AI check itself.

2. Plan for surprises

What happens when something unexpected comes in? Build a fallback for every decision point. When in doubt, the AI sends it to a human.

3. Train your team
1
Update your written processes to include the AI steps
2
Record a 5-minute video showing the new workflow
3
Pick one person per department as the AI champion
4
Roll out one workflow at a time, not ten

Watch Out

Common Mistakes

Mistake 1: Not writing things down+

"We know our processes." No, you don't. Write them down. You'll find steps you didn't know existed.

Mistake 2: Automating the fun stuff first+

Automate the boring, repetitive tasks nobody wants to do. That's where the money is.

Mistake 3: Not checking AI's work+

AI makes mistakes. Confidently. One bad email to a client can cost more than the whole project saved.

Mistake 4: Doing everything at once+

One workflow at a time. Get it stable. Then add the next.

Mistake 5: Expecting magic+

AI needs good instructions. Bad inputs = bad outputs. Always.

Mistake 6: Ignoring your team+

Tell your team AI removes the boring parts of their job, not their job. Involve them early.

Mistake 7: Not measuring results+

Track time saved, errors reduced, and capacity gained. Every month.

A-Tier Claude

A-Tier: Claude Desktop (with Projects)

Claude is a better LLM than ChatGPT for business. The reasoning is sharper, it follows instructions more precisely, and it doesn't make up as much stuff.

What makes it A-tier:

Projects. You create a project, upload your documents (SOPs, brand guidelines, pricing, whatever), and Claude remembers all of it across every conversation.

What we use it for:
Writing marketing copy
that matches our brand voice
Reviewing and editing content
Analysing data and spreadsheets
Brainstorming strategy with
full context on our business
Drafting emails, proposals,
and client communications
How to set it up:
1
Go to claude.ai and sign up ($20/month USD for Pro)
2
Click "Projects" in the sidebar
3
Create a new project (e.g. "Marketing" or "Client Proposals")
4
Upload your key documents: brand guide, SOPs, pricing, FAQs, past work examples
5
Start a conversation inside the project. Claude now knows everything you uploaded

B And C Tier

B-Tier: Claude Desktop (Without Integrations)

Claude on its own, without Projects or any document uploads, is still better than ChatGPT for business tasks. The reasoning is stronger.

How to upgrade from B to A: Create Projects. Upload your key docs. That's it. That one step moves Claude from "good chatbot" to "team member who knows everything."

C-Tier: ChatGPT (With Projects)

ChatGPT with projects and integrations is usable. It's fine. Millions of people use it and get value from it.

Why it's C-tier and not higher:
The reasoning isn't
as strong as Claude for business tasks

clunky compared to Claude's
It's trying to
do everything (image gen, browsing, code, voice) instead...

D And E Tier

D-Tier: Atlas

Atlas is basically a ChatGPT-powered browser. You ask it to research something and it browses the web for you.

Why it's D-tier: It's a fancy search engine. You can do the same thing with Perplexity or just using Claude with web access. There's no unique value here that justifies another subscription.

E-Tier: Perplexity

Perplexity is a search engine with AI summaries. It's fine for quick research but it doesn't DO anything for your business.

Why it's E-tier: It solves one problem (research) that other A-tier tools already solve as a side feature.

E-Tier: Microsoft Copilot

This is the one that hurts to say because so many businesses are on the Microsoft stack.

Why it's E-tier:
It's slow
The AI quality
is noticeably worse than Claude or even ChatGPT
The integrations feel
bolted on, not native
It costs more
per user than Claude Pro
The Teams integration
is mediocre at best
